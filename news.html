Life at Busy Bees, as it happens.

Successful Stories & Achievements

Recognition we’re proud of.

Innovation
Image

9 August 2019

MasterCard Foundation Innovation Exhibition

At Kacyiru’s Kigali Heights Building, our pupils presented ICT-driven innovations: a hand water pump, a pneumatic door, Scratch programming for children, model cars and airplanes, guitars, and a basketball ground design.

Sport

2019

Never-Again 25 Karate Champions

Our Karate teams won the trophy and two medals at the Never-Again 25 Karate Competitions, held at Rwanda National Stadium Amahoro in Remera.

Award

2018

Education Quality Award - 1st in Africa

BBFS won the 2018 Education Quality Award Program by Opportunity International, taking First Prize in the Africa-wide celebration.

Timeline

Moments from our school calendar.

01

2025–2026

Community Outreach - Giheka Village ECD

In line with our 3H approach (Head, Heart and Hand), BBFS learners visited Giheka Village ECD to support children affected by malnutrition. With their parents’ support, learners contributed small donations used to purchase milk, eggs and fruit - a joyful, impactful experience that fostered empathy and social responsibility.

02

2025–2026

School Open Day

A vibrant Open Day gave parents and the wider community the opportunity to witness the academic achievements, skills and talents of our pupils.

03

2025–2026

Friendly Volleyball Match

BBFS hosted a friendly volleyball match with a neighbouring school, bringing teachers together in a spirit of teamwork, unity and shared enjoyment rather than competition.

04

2025–2026

Educational Trip to Nyirangarama

As part of our experiential learning approach, we planned visits to a farm and a juice-processing industry at Nyirangarama, giving learners practical exposure to agriculture and entrepreneurship.

05

2025–2026

Partnership Engagement - Edify Rwanda

BBFS hosted visitors from our partner, Edify Rwanda, sharing our background, achievements and growth journey, with discussions on how their capacity-building programmes have shaped the school’s future direction.

06

2025–2026

Learners’ Growth & Life Skills Sessions

Interactive sessions grouped learners by age (8–14 years) to guide them through personal growth and body development, with boys mentored by male teachers and girls by female teachers in a safe, supportive environment.

07

2025–2026

ITORERO Club - Cultural Values & Identity

Through IBIGANIRO discussions, pupils deepened their understanding of Rwandan values and identity: Ndi Umunyarwanda, Umuganda, Umuntu, Patriotism, Indangagaciro and the philosophy of Ubuntu - “I am because we are.”

08

17 November 2023

School Opening Day

Pupils showcased skills from Science, ICT, Environment, Art, Culture and Fellowship clubs to parents, neighbours, school leaders and teachers from other schools - and were interviewed by Flash TV journalists.

09

1 March 2024

Pupils Talent Show

Pupils presented talents across sport, music, fashion, science, art, journalism and dancing, with gifts awarded to winners in front of a thrilled audience of parents and teachers.

10

21 May 2024

Sport Competition - Volleyball

Esther House beat Debora House in the girls’ final; Joshua House beat David House in the boys’. The event promoted fitness, teamwork, discipline and healthy competition.

11

7 June 2024

Educational Trip to Nyandungu Eco-Park

The nursery section and lower primary explored the Nyandungu Eco-Park in Gasabo District, discovering trees and birds in a serene, nature-based setting.

12

9 June 2024

Educational Trip to Huye

Pupils visited the Rwanda Cultural Heritage Academy Ethnographic Museum in Huye District, exploring exhibits of Rwandan culture, history and ancient traditions, dances and music.

13

Weekly, every Tuesday

Staff Peer Learning

Teachers meet weekly to develop consistent handwriting techniques, build lesson plans collaboratively, and discuss class management, the SINIDI technique, learner assessment and reading/writing fluency strategies.

14

2023–2024

School Closure Event

A grand celebration marked the graduation of Nursery 3 pupils to Primary and Primary 6 pupils to Secondary level, with theatre, science and art performances, prayers and thanksgiving.

15

15 September 2019

Tour: University of Rwanda & National Museum

Pupils and staff explored the University’s biodiversity and herbarium (plants preserved since 1915), then visited the Institute of National Museum of Rwanda, built in 1987, with seven galleries of ethnographic, artistic, historical and archaeological artifacts.

16

19 June 2019

Agriculture & Livestock Exhibition, Murindi

Pupils and staff had a great chance to see different animals and crops at the exhibition.

17

27 August 2019

Modern Charcoal Cooker Demonstration

Staff and parents learned about a modern charcoal cooker from Geni Green Solution Ltd; the school and several teachers purchased cookers for their families.

18

23 August 2019

Umuganura Celebration

The school celebrated Umuganura, teaching learners the materials, meaning and history of the harvest festival - building relationships, friendship and happiness among pupils.
